# Partnership Scorecard
## Structured Evaluation Framework for Co-Founders & Strategic Partners

**Problem Solved:** The "Partnership/Leap" phase is often vague ("depends on energy and connections"). This scorecard provides structure.

**Created by:** Reviewer enhancement to Z.I.N.O.K methodology

---

## üéØ When to Use This

**Use for:**
- Evaluating potential co-founders
- Assessing strategic partners
- Board member selection
- Key hire decisions
- Investor alignment

**Don't use for:**
- Employees (different criteria)
- Contractors (short-term relationships)
- Advisors (lower commitment)
- Customers (different goals)

## üìä The Complete Scorecard

### Section 1: Skills & Capabilities (30 points)

**Complementary Skills (10 points)**
- [ ] **10pts:** Fills critical gap (you're tech, they're sales)
- [ ] **7pts:** Partially complementary (some overlap, some new)
- [ ] **4pts:** Similar skills (redundant but useful)
- [ ] **0pts:** Identical skills (no added value)

**Track Record (10 points)**
- [ ] **10pts:** Successfully built/scaled before
- [ ] **7pts:** Relevant experience, no exits
- [ ] **4pts:** Adjacent experience
- [ ] **0pts:** First-time, unproven

**Execution Speed (10 points)**
- [ ] **10pts:** Ships daily, moves fast
- [ ] **7pts:** Consistent weekly progress
- [ ] **4pts:** Slow but steady
- [ ] **0pts:** Analysis paralysis, talks more than does

**Subtotal: ___/30**

---

### Section 2: Network & Resources (30 points)

**Existing Network (10 points)**
- [ ] **10pts:** Direct access to 100+ relevant contacts
- [ ] **7pts:** Strong network, needs activation
- [ ] **4pts:** Some connections, building
- [ ] **0pts:** Starting from zero

**Financial Contribution (10 points)**
- [ ] **10pts:** Can fund runway (6+ months)
- [ ] **7pts:** Meaningful investment ($25K+)
- [ ] **4pts:** Symbolic investment ($5-10K)
- [ ] **0pts:** No capital available

**Unfair Advantage (10 points)**
- [ ] **10pts:** Unique access impossible to replicate (distribution, IP, relationships)
- [ ] **7pts:** Significant advantage, others could build
- [ ] **4pts:** Modest advantage, somewhat unique
- [ ] **0pts:** No unique advantage

**Subtotal: ___/30**

---

### Section 3: Alignment & Fit (40 points)

**Timeline Alignment (10 points)**
- [ ] **10pts:** Same urgency (both full-time, aggressive)
- [ ] **7pts:** Mostly aligned (both committed, similar pace)
- [ ] **4pts:** Different speeds (one faster than other)
- [ ] **0pts:** Mismatched (one urgent, one casual)

**Risk Tolerance (10 points)**
- [ ] **10pts:** Identical risk appetite
- [ ] **7pts:** Similar enough to not cause friction
- [ ] **4pts:** Different but workable
- [ ] **0pts:** Fundamental mismatch (one wants stability, other wants moonshot)

**Cultural Fit (10 points)**
- [ ] **10pts:** Share values, communication style, work ethic
- [ ] **7pts:** Different but respectful
- [ ] **4pts:** Some friction, manageable
- [ ] **0pts:** Clash on fundamentals

**Skin in the Game (10 points)**
- [ ] **10pts:** Full-time commitment + invested capital
- [ ] **7pts:** Full-time OR significant capital
- [ ] **4pts:** Part-time + small investment
- [ ] **0pts:** No commitment (wants equity for ideas)

**Subtotal: ___/40**

## üö© Automatic Disqualifiers (Red Flags)

Even with high score, these are deal-breakers:

### Red Flags - Behavior
- [ ] **"Ideas person"** who won't execute ("I'll be the visionary, you build")
- [ ] **Wants 50/50 equity** but contributes 20% effort
- [ ] **No skin in the game** (time or money)
- [ ] **Different risk tolerance** (fundamental mismatch)
- [ ] **Unclear role boundaries** ("we'll figure it out later")
- [ ] **Poor communication** (disappears for days, vague responses)
- [ ] **Conflict avoidance** (agrees to everything, then doesn't deliver)
- [ ] **Credit-seeking** (takes credit for others' work)

### Red Flags - Capability
- [ ] **Never shipped** (chronic starter, never finisher)
- [ ] **Can't focus** (always chasing new ideas)
- [ ] **Ego-driven** (can't take feedback)
- [ ] **Inflexible** (my way or highway)
- [ ] **Avoids hard work** (only wants "fun" parts)

### Red Flags - Integrity
- [ ] **Lies or exaggerates** (track record, connections, capabilities)
- [ ] **Unethical behavior** (even in small things)
- [ ] **Speaks poorly** of past partners/employers
- [ ] **Blame others** (never their fault)
- [ ] **Commitmentphobe** (always keeps options open)

**If ANY red flag present ‚Üí Score = 0, Do Not Proceed**

## üí∞ Equity Split Framework

**Don't default to 50/50.** Base on contribution:

### Contribution Factors

**Idea (5-10%)**
- Original concept
- Market research
- Problem validation

**Execution (25-40%)**
- Who builds?
- Who sells?
- Who operates?

**Capital (15-30%)**
- Who funds?
- How much?
- When needed?

**Risk (10-20%)**
- Who quits job?
- Who has dependents?
- Who has runway?

**Domain Expertise (10-25%)**
- Industry connections
- Technical skills
- Unique advantage

### Example Splits

**Scenario 1: Technical + Business**
- You: 40% execution (build) + 10% capital + 15% risk = 65%
- Them: 30% execution (sales) + 5% idea = 35%

**Scenario 2: Equal Partners**
- You: 30% execution + 10% capital + 15% risk + 10% expertise = 65%
- Them: 35% execution + 5% capital + 5% idea + 5% expertise = 50%
- Adjust: 50/50 if truly equal

**Scenario 3: Late Joiner**
- You (6 months in): 50% (already built MVP, have customers)
- Them (joining now): 15-25% (bringing sales/scale expertise)
- Pool for future team: 25-35%

### Vesting Schedule

**Standard:** 4 years, 1-year cliff
- Earn 25% at 1 year
- Earn 1/48th monthly thereafter
- Leaves early? Keep only what vested

**Accelerated:** 3 years for fast-moving startups
**Cliff:** 6-12 months (test period)

**No vesting = huge mistake.** Always vest.

## üìã Partnership Agreement Checklist

**Document these BEFORE starting:**

**Roles & Responsibilities:**
- [ ] Who builds what?
- [ ] Who talks to customers?
- [ ] Who handles ops/admin?
- [ ] What if roles change?

**Decision-Making:**
- [ ] Who has final say on what?
- [ ] How do we break ties?
- [ ] What needs unanimous vs majority?

**Time Commitment:**
- [ ] Full-time or part-time?
- [ ] Minimum hours/week?
- [ ] What if can't meet commitment?

**Financial:**
- [ ] Equity split (with rationale)
- [ ] Vesting schedule
- [ ] Who invests how much?
- [ ] Salary (if any) timing?

**Exit Scenarios:**
- [ ] What if someone wants out?
- [ ] Buyout terms?
- [ ] Non-compete?
- [ ] IP ownership?

**Get it in writing.** Use SAFE or simple partnership agreement.

## üéØ Trial Period Approach

**Don't commit immediately.** Run a trial:

**Month 1: Test Project**
- Small, defined scope
- Both contribute equally
- See how you work together
- No equity discussions yet

**Month 2: Evaluate**
- Communication: Easy or frustrating?
- Execution: Do they deliver?
- Chemistry: Enjoy working together?
- Values: Aligned on how to build?

**Month 3: Decision**
- Complete the scorecard
- Check for red/green flags
- Discuss equity openly
- Formalize or part ways

**Better to discover misfit in 3 months than 3 years.**

## üìä Case Study: Good vs Bad Partnership

### ‚ùå Bad Partnership (Real Example)

**Setup:**
- Solo founder (technical)
- Brings on "business partner"
- 50/50 split, no vesting

**Red Flags Ignored:**
- Partner had no network (claimed to have it)
- Never made a sale (said "just need time")
- Part-time commitment (had other project)
- Equity without vesting (left at 8 months)

**Outcome:**
- Partner left, kept 50% equity
- Solo founder had to buy back or dilute
- 12 months wasted
- Nearly killed company

**Scorecard would have shown: 35/100 (RED FLAG)**

### ‚úÖ Good Partnership (Real Example)

**Setup:**
- Solo founder (technical)
- Brings on sales lead from industry
- 60/40 split, 4-year vesting

**Green Flags:**
- Direct access to 50+ decision-makers
- Track record (sold similar product before)
- Full-time from day 1
- Invested $25K own money

**Outcome:**
- First customer in 2 weeks (from network)
- $500K ARR in 6 months
- Complementary skills (no overlap or conflict)
- Still together 3 years later

**Scorecard would have shown: 85/100 (STRONG YES)**

## üîÑ Annual Partnership Review

**Every 12 months, re-evaluate:**

**Contribution Check:**
- Who delivered what?
- Equal effort vs equity split?
- Adjust compensation if imbalanced?

**Alignment Check:**
- Still want same things?
- Same timeline?
- Same risk tolerance?

**Red Flag Check:**
- Any new concerns?
- Communication issues?
- Performance problems?

**Options:**
- Continue as-is (if aligned)
- Adjust roles (if capabilities changed)
- Buy out (if misaligned)
- Bring in new partner (if gaps)

**Startups change. Partnerships should adapt.**
